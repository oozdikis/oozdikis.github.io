Biography
I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Department of Computer Science at Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU) in Trondheim. I received my PhD degree in computer science from the Middle East Technical University (METU) in 2016. My research interests mainly include machine learning, data mining, spatio-temporal data analysis, big data and semantic web technologies. I am recently working on location prediction in social networks, specifically estimating the geographical locations for tweets and events. I presented the results of my latest work at several information retrieval conferences, including SIGIR'18 in Michigan and ECIR'18 in France.

I also have professional working experience at several finance and telecommunication companies in the industry, including 5 years as a solutions architect at Garanti Bank BBVA and 4 years as a software engineer at Siemens. I am mainly specialized in service and data integration, big data architectures, enterprise services and service-oriented architectures. I participated in several R&D projects, in which I designed and developed applications for information extraction, semantic web, augmented reality, and geographical information systems.
